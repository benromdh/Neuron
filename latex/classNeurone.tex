\subsection{Detailed Description}
A class that simulate 1 Neuron. A neuron is mainly caracterized by it's membrane potential and the number of \char`\"{}spikes\char`\"{} it makes during it's lifetime \-: indeed, the potential membrane is incremented at each step until it reaches a certain limit value, or threshold \-: then the potential membrane is set to 0 back again and the spike and the time it occured are recorded. As we'll simulate a network of neurons, each neuron has it's own targets, that will receive a certain amount of imput Jext or Jin from the \char`\"{}firing neuron\char`\"{} after a certain delay, depending on the nature of the neuron \-: This one can be either excitatory or inhibitory. The buffer object is a vector that will make sure that the neuron receives the imput received arrives after a certain delay.

For the time of the simulation(ms), we'll be mainly working with steps(t) \-: a step of time is equal to a time interval(unit time) of 0.\-1 ms. Thus the global time(\-T) is obtained with the relation \-: T = t$\ast$h when needed. The membrane potential and so J, are recorded in (m\-V).
